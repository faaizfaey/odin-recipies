Fried Chicken

Description

This is the only crispy fried chicken recipe you'll ever need. Why? It combines all the tried-and-true tricks to ensure the juiciest, most flavorful, crispiest chicken every time.

Most fried chicken is cooked at a high temperature throughout the frying process. This one, however, only starts at a very high heat â€” after browning, the heat is reduced for about 30 minutes. You'll turn up the temperature again at the end, locking in that crispy texture.

Paprika adds smoky flavor and has a low smoke point, which helps with browning the chicken.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken
  • Buttermilk
  • Flour
  • Seasonings
  • Oil

Steps:

  1. Take you cut up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer.
  2. Put the flour in a large plastic bag. Season the flour with paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk, after some time put them in the bag with the flour, seal the bag and shake it to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper.Let sit until the flour is of a paste like consistency.
  5. Fill a large skillet about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until very hot
  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in hot oil on both sides.
  7. When browned, reduce the heat and cover the skillet; let cook for 30 minutes. Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.
  8. Drain the fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have to fry in a few shifts.
